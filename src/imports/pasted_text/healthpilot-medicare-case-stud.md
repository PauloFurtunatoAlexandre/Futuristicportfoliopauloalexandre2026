Simplifying the Complex: Supporting Better Decision-Making—Empowering Users with Medicare Plans
Navigating healthcare insurance for seniors can feel like solving a complex puzzle—frustrating, time-consuming, and overwhelming. HealthPilot’s platform aimed to simplify this process but was falling short. Users reported confusion, drop-offs during enrollment, and a lack of trust in their final decisions. My role was to transform this experience into something intuitive, empowering, and user-friendly.

Problem
It was clear that users were struggling to complete the insurance enrollment process, often abandoning the platform out of frustration.

While the issue was flagged by the product team, I took the initiative to dig deeper. I conducted user interviews and usability tests to get to the heart of the matter. The biggest culprits? Confusing navigation, overwhelming jargon, and a lack of clear guidance.

The goal was simple but crucial: make the platform easier to use and more supportive for our users. My starting strategy was to fully understand the user experience, map out where things were going wrong, and prioritize solutions that would have the most impact.

This was a big deal. If we didn’t fix the issues, users would continue to feel unsupported, hurting both their trust in the platform and the company’s bottom line. But if we got it right, we’d not only improve their experience but also build confidence and loyalty in the process.

Solution
Created a product and iterated into high-fidelity prototypes, focusing on:
- Simplified Language: Replaced jargon with clear, user-friendly terms.
- Step-by-Step Guidance: Added progress indicators and contextual tips.
- Plan Comparison: Introduced side-by-side comparisons with clear pros and cons.

Resulting in:

6% Increase in Plan Selection Rates: Users were now able to find and choose plans with ease and confidence.

25% Reduction in Drop-Off Rates: Simplified navigation and clear guidance kept users engaged throughout the enrollment process.

97% User Satisfaction: Post-launch surveys showed an overwhelming majority of users found the platform intuitive and supportive.

51% Usability Improvement: Iterative testing and user-focused refinements significantly enhanced the overall user experience.

$10 Million Investment Secured: The successful redesign helped demonstrate the platform’s potential to investors, resulting in a new funding round that further solidified HealthPilot’s growth trajectory.

Conducting Research
User Interviews: I conducted 15 interviews with a mix of new and returning users. These conversations revealed consistent frustrations: users struggled to find relevant information, felt overwhelmed by complex insurance jargon, and lacked confidence in their decisions due to minimal guidance.

Surveys: A survey of 349 users provided valuable quantitative insights, with 70% highlighting significant difficulties when searching for Medicare plans. This data helped quantify the main pain points, such as confusion and navigation challenges, giving us a clear picture of where the experience was falling short. To see our survey results, click here.

Usability Testing: Watching users interact with the platform in real-time revealed exactly where they were getting stuck, such as comparing plans or understanding key terms.

To complement these insights, I conducted a competitive analysis, desk research, and a deep product discovery phase. This broader perspective allowed me to benchmark HealthPilot against similar platforms, identify best practices, and explore opportunities to differentiate our user experience. These additional research methods provided valuable context and actionable insights, helping us refine our approach. Some of the insights can be found by clicking here.



Defining the Problem
After collecting insights, I mapped out the user journey and pinpointed the most significant friction points. Using a dot-voting session with the product team (Business Stakeholders and PMs), we prioritized tackling these core issues first, focusing on simplicity and user confidence.

Navigation Confusion: Users couldn’t locate key features or understand the enrollment flow.

Complex Jargon: Insurance terms like “deductible” and “coinsurance” were unclear to many.

No Guidance: A lack of visual cues left users feeling unsupported and uncertain about next steps.

Finding the Right Solution
With the problem clearly defined, I moved into the second diamond (and repeated it with more iterations and learnings): exploring and testing potential solutions.


Generating Ideas
Design Studio Workshop: I facilitated a collaborative session with stakeholders, generating over 15 ideas for simplifying the user experience. Ideas ranged from reworking navigation to adding progress indicators and contextual tips.

Impact/Effort Matrix: To focus our efforts, we evaluated each idea based on its potential impact and implementation effort, prioritizing high-impact, low-effort changes like simplifying navigation and adding step-by-step guidance.



Prototyping and Testing
Wireframes: I created low-fidelity wireframes for a new, streamlined user flow. To see the first wireframes I did, click here.

High-Fidelity Prototypes: These included:
- Simplified Language: Replacing jargon with plain, user-friendly terms.
- Step-by-Step Guidance: Adding progress indicators and tips to guide users.
- Plan Comparison: Introducing side-by-side comparisons with clear pros and cons for each plan.

User Testing: We conducted remote moderated testing with 10 participants. Results showed a 90% success rate, with users praising the improved clarity and usability of the platform. To see the firsts tests I did using the first ideas we had in our team, click here.

Challenges and Results
Challenges and How I Overcame Them
Achieving these results came with its share of challenges, each of which pushed me to find thoughtful and creative solutions:

Decoding Insurance Jargon:
Simplifying complex terminology was essential to improving usability but had to be handled with care to maintain accuracy. By collaborating closely with subject matter experts, I created user-friendly language that was clear, approachable, and trustworthy—a change that users highly appreciated.

Stakeholder Buy-In:
Aligning teams with competing priorities required tact and strategic communication. I used storytelling to highlight the direct connection between user research findings, improved satisfaction, and business goals. This approach helped create a shared vision, ensuring alignment across all teams.

Time Constraints:
With only four months to deliver the first version of the product, I focused on features that would make the most immediate impact. By adopting an iterative process, I balanced speed with quality, continuously validating solutions through user testing to ensure we were on the right track.

These challenges weren’t just obstacles—they were opportunities to grow as a designer, refine my approach, and prove the value of thoughtful UX design.



Results
The redesign of HealthPilot’s platform delivered transformative outcomes:

6% Increase in Plan Selection Rates: Users were now able to find and choose plans with ease and confidence.

25% Reduction in Drop-Off Rates: Simplified navigation and clear guidance kept users engaged throughout the enrollment process.

97% User Satisfaction: Post-launch surveys showed an overwhelming majority of users found the platform intuitive and supportive.

51% Usability Improvement: Iterative testing and user-focused refinements significantly enhanced the overall user experience.

$10 Million Investment Secured: The successful redesign helped demonstrate the platform’s potential to investors, resulting in a new funding round that further solidified HealthPilot’s growth trajectory.

These achievements underscored the power of a user-centered design approach in creating meaningful impact—not just for users but for the business as well.



Next Steps
Building on the success of the HealthPilot redesign, the next steps focus on continuing to enhance the user experience and leveraging new opportunities:

Implement Personalized Recommendations: Develop a dynamic recommendation system that uses user behavior data to suggest the most relevant insurance plans, further simplifying the decision-making process.

Expand Support Features: Introduce live chat functionality to provide real-time assistance and address any remaining user uncertainties during the enrollment process.

Monitor Post-Launch Metrics: Continuously track key metrics such as plan selection rates, drop-off rates, and user satisfaction to identify new pain points and areas for refinement.

Iterate on Design: Use ongoing user feedback and usability testing to ensure the platform evolves in alignment with changing user needs and expectations.
