Designing Emotion-Driven Shopping Experiences: How I Helped to Boost Customer Loyalty for Rise Cannabis
Finding the right cannabis product can often feel confusing, with users struggling to match their desired effects to the perfect product. Rise Cannabis set out to solve this challenge by creating an emotion-driven shopping experience. However, the existing platform left many users frustrated, leading to missed opportunities for engagement and loyalty. My role was to redesign this experience into something intuitive, personalized, and educational, ensuring users could effortlessly find products that aligned with their moods and preferences.

Problem
Shopping for cannabis isn’t just about products—it’s about emotions. Customers at Rise weren’t just looking for an edible or flower; they were looking for relaxation after a long day, creativity for their next project, or relief from pain. But the online experience wasn’t delivering. Users found it frustrating to translate what they wanted to feel into the right product, leaving them confused, disengaged, and less likely to return.

The challenge was clear: how could we bridge this emotional gap and turn product browsing into a meaningful, intuitive journey? With competition heating up in the cannabis space, the stakes were more than just financial—they were about building trust, loyalty, and a brand identity that resonated deeply with customers. It wasn’t enough to fix the problem; we had to reimagine how people shopped for cannabis online.

Solution
Using insights from UX research in the first diamond of the design process, I moved into the solution phase with a structured approach grounded in the double-diamond methodology. Through a collaborative workshop with stakeholders, we generated 8+ ideas and prioritized them using an impact/effort matrix, focusing on tools that could address emotional shopping needs effectively. The top solutions were the "Fit Guide," which aligned products with user moods, and the "Feelings in Reviews" filter, enabling customers to sort by emotional experiences shared by other users.

I created wireframes and iterated into high-fidelity prototypes, emphasizing simplified navigation, emotion-centric filters, and educational touchpoints to enhance confidence in product selection. Usability testing revealed a 60% success rate in users finding products aligned with their desired effects. While some advanced features were postponed due to resource constraints, the tools successfully redefined the shopping journey, turning a complex process into an intuitive and emotionally resonant experience.


My Role

Challenges and Results
Challenges and Trade-offs
Balancing innovation with scalability required close collaboration with developers to ensure the tools integrated seamlessly into the existing architecture.

Some advanced filtering features were postponed to maintain focus on immediate user needs, with plans for future iterations.

Educating internal teams about the emotional approach to design required workshops to align goals and expectations.

This structured and user-focused approach transformed the Rise Cannabis shopping experience, turning product discovery into a journey that resonated emotionally with users.


Result
61% Increase in Product Matches: The redesigned tools, including the "Fit Guide" and "Feelings in Reviews" filter, helped users find products aligned with their desired effects more efficiently, enhancing satisfaction and confidence.

44% Growth in User Engagement: Users spent more time exploring products, thanks to the emotionally driven navigation and educational touchpoints, leading to deeper engagement with the platform.

52% Surge in Repeat Customers: The seamless shopping journey, from discovery to post-purchase, resulted in increased customer loyalty and repeat visits.

Revenue Growth: The improvements contributed to Green Thumb Industries' Q3 2023 revenue of $275 million, a 9% sequential increase, showcasing the impact of a refined user experience.

Positive feedback from users who described the platform as more "intuitive" and "easy to navigate."

The project also introduced new UX practices within the team, emphasizing user-centric design and iterative testing to solve complex customer pain points.


Learnings & Next Steps
Learnings:

The Power of Emotions: Integrating emotional cues into design can significantly improve user satisfaction and drive engagement.

Cross-Team Collaboration: Close collaboration with stakeholders and developers was key to aligning innovative ideas with technical feasibility and business goals.

Next Steps:

Expand the "Feelings in Reviews" filter with additional data points to refine recommendations further.

Introduce advanced filtering options for more tailored user experiences.

Conduct A/B testing on the cart and checkout flows to identify additional optimization opportunities.

This project solidified the importance of designing for both functional and emotional user needs, creating a shopping experience that resonates on a personal level.
